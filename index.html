Shrimad Bhagavad Gita

The Divine Song of God

The Bhagavad Gita is a 700-verse Hindu scripture that is part of the epic Mahabharata. Set on the battlefield of Kurukshetra, it captures the timeless dialogue between Prince Arjuna and Lord Krishna, offering profound wisdom on duty, righteousness, and the path to self-realization.

The Divine Authors

Maharishi Ved Vyasa

The Compiler

Maharishi Ved Vyasa, born as Krishna Dvaipayana, was the son of Sage Parashara and Satyavati. He is regarded as a Chiranjivi and the architect of Vedic knowledge. He classified the Vedas into four and authored the Mahabharata. Uniquely, he was both the grandfather of the Pandavas and Kauravas and the narrator of their story. He dictated the epic to Lord Ganesha and granted divine vision to Sanjaya. His presence ensured the preservation of Dharma for future generations.

Lord Ganesha

The Divine Scribe

Lord Ganesha, the son of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati, is revered as the Vighnaharta or remover of obstacles and the patron of intellect and wisdom. In the context of the Mahabharata, he played the indispensable role of the divine scribe. When Sage Ved Vyasa conceived the vast epic, he required a writer capable of matching his depth. Ganesha accepted on the condition that Vyasa would not pause. To fulfill this, he broke his own tusk to use as a stylus, symbolizing sacrifice for sacred knowledge. His role transformed the epic from oral tradition into a timeless written masterpiece.

Frequently Asked Questions

Clearing common doubts about the Shrimad Bhagavad Gita.

What is the main message of the Bhagavad Gita?

The core message is Nishkama Karma—performing one's duty selflessly without attachment to the results, while remaining equanimous in both success and failure.

Is the Gita a religious book or a philosophical guide?

While it is a sacred Hindu scripture, it is widely regarded as a universal philosophical guide. It addresses human psychology, ethics, and the nature of existence, making it relevant to people of all faiths.

Why was the Gita spoken on a battlefield?

The battlefield of Kurukshetra symbolizes the internal conflict we face daily. It teaches that spirituality isn't about escaping life, but about finding clarity and strength in the midst of life's toughest challenges.

Who wrote the Bhagavad Gita?

The Gita is part of the Mahabharata, traditionally attributed to the sage Vyasa. It records the dialogue between Sri Krishna and Arjuna.

How many chapters and verses are in the Gita?

The Gita consists of 18 Chapters and 700 Verses (Shlokas).

Can the Gita help with modern-day stress?

Yes. By teaching us to focus on the present moment and detach from "what-if" anxieties, it acts as a powerful manual for mental health and resilience.

What is the difference between Yoga in the Gita and physical Yoga?

In the Gita, 'Yoga' means Union with the Divine or excellence in action. While physical postures (Asanas) are a small part, the Gita focuses on Yoga of Knowledge (Jnana), Devotion (Bhakti), and Action (Karma).

Does the Gita support violence?

No. It supports Dharma (Righteousness). Krishna urges Arjuna to fight not out of anger or hatred, but as a duty to protect justice and stop systemic evil when all peaceful options have failed.

Which commentary of the Gita should I start with?

It depends on your interest. For devotion, try Prabhupada; for meditation, Eknath Easwaran; and for logical philosophy, Swami Chinmayananda.

What is 'Dharma' in the context of the Gita?

Dharma is the essential nature or the "right path" of a being. It refers to the duties that sustain the individual and society in harmony with the cosmic order.
